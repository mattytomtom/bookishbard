"A Festival of Heroes"

Took place 6-13-26, in-game Tarsakh 1st 1487

*Our heroes meet new faces, try new things, and revel in the festival dedicated to adventurers. But the life of adventurers are known to be as dangerous as they are rewarding. . . would a festival in their honor be any different?*

The session starts in the afternoon of Tarsakh 1, amidst the chaos involving [[Ysgraviir]], [[Forge]], and [[Ven]] at the Wide-Eyed Willow. Chairs broken, twigs snapped, and [[Lady Sarya Crownsilver]] absent, Purple Dragon Knights appear on the scene. [[Ven]] immediately surrenders; the feelings of [[Ysgraviir]] and [[Forge]] are not immediately apparent, as the establishment's employees have polymorphed them into sheep. Nevertheless, the trio is promptly manacled and perp walked to the dungeons, where they are chained to cots in the same cell.

- [[Rook]] seeks out [[Ember]]. Finding her at the Temple to Mystra, they set off to wander around [[Suzail]] to take in the sights of the festival. At one point, [[Ember]] overhears a shady pair whispering about her "knowing about The Hand". The married duo elect to follow the whisperers to the Temple of the God of Thieves. Inside, they find the place vacant. After inspecting the statue to the Temple's God, [[Rook]] and [[Ember]] figure out how to uncover a secret passage through their combined knowledge of Thieves' Cant. This newly revealed dual stairway gives them the option to travel to an area that reeks of blood, or one that. . . doesn't reek of blood. They choose the latter. At the end of the stairs, they happen across a large table, where several folks sit; some chatting, some playing cards. The pair's arrival triggers stares all around, which is quickly ameliorated by [[Rook]] utilizing Cant to convey their peaceful intent. Approaching the table, [[Ember]] offers to pay for information, but is refused, as the inhabitants at the table would rather play cards for it. [[Rook]] and [[Ember]] acquiesce, though [[Rook]] readies a knife in case things go south. In the midst of the card game, [[Ember]] voices her desire to know more about the Red Right Hand, which prompts glares and shushes all around. She is advised that The Hand are a particularly mean group of slavery, traffickers, murderers, essentially all the bad -ers that a group could possibly be. Brief mention is also made of The Blue Dragons loosing their footing in the coastal trade. [[Ember]] is told she knows to much; or, at least, she asks too much, as the Priestess of the Temple of Mystra evidently tattled on her asking about The Hand. The card player lets [[Rook]] and [[Ember]] know that they're probably both on The Hands' list of people to make disappear, and it would behoove them to not go poking around. The thieves are of the opinion that the information they've shared is worth a favor in return; [[Ember]] takes slight offense to this, and the pair leave.

- We come upon the docks of [[Suzail]]. A tall(ish) copper Dragonborn, flecked with green and black and wearing a clerical robe, comes into view. [[Tharrick]] Fellwing just shipped in, and needs some gear. Needing an adventurer's pack, [[Tharrick]] approaches a merchant that looks suspiciously like a smurf. Succeeding in that, the Dragon born stops by the stand of a fellow with an incredible mustache, seeking some plate armor. Sir 'Stache is downright extortionate in his pricing, so [[Tharrick]] NAMEDROPS THE RED HAND, getting a discount on the condition that he leaves the merchant the heck alone. Now needing a weapon, [[Tharrick]] happens across an Orc providing demonstrations of his wares' proficiency, breaking a small crossbow in the process. Upon being asked what he recommends, the Orc advises [[Tharrick]] to buy a big ol' Longbow. After doing some solid target shooting for a discount, [[Tharrick]] also buys a Longsword (with complimentary whetstone) and is on his way. To cap things off, he seeks a shield. [[Tharrick]] tracks down a seller that can outfit shields with holy symbols, allowing for use in spells. He chooses the symbol of Eldath and makes the purchase. Freshly outfitted, [[Tharrick]] asks a nearby Purple Dragon Knight where he can get some food and rest, and is pointed to The [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]]. The Knight just finished their shift, and joins [[Tharrick]] in the walk. After resisting the wiles of [[Callie O'Toole]], they eat and chat for a bit, ending with [[Tharrick]] paying for both of their meals, which touches the Knight. The Dragonborn then spots a cloaked figure staring daggers, and goes to approach. Pulling up a chair, [[Tharrick]] introduces himself and inquires why the staring. Turns out Mr. Cloak is a full blown racist, prompting [[Tharrick]] to mentally flip a coin: heads, punch a racist; tails, let it slide.

  The coin lands heads up.

  A glorious tavern brawl is initiated as [[Tharrick]] grabs the racist by the collar, decks him in the face, and permission seems to be granted to everyone in the [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]] to do likewise to whomever is nearby. The fighting goes on for a bit, complete with the time-honored trope of a fellow being dragged across the bartop, until a guard show up and tries to restore order. The racist attempts to flee, but an airborne barstool put him to sleep near the door. [[Tharrick]] seeks respite from the violence, and leaves the bar.

- [[Vaelithra]] is passing by The [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]] with [[Tanreth Saer]], only to collide with a fleeing [[Tharrick]]. [[Vaelithra]] chooses violence and shoves him back into the tavern. Promptly re-exiting, [[Tharrick]] inquires what the heck that was for, and is met with incredulous looks. Utilizing the Booming Voice effect of Thaumaturgy, [[Vaelithra]] tells the bar to shut up, and the inhabitants comply. Quick introductions are made; [[Tharrick]] explains his part in the fight, [[Vaelithra]] offers friendship (which [[Tharrick]] accepts), and [[Tanreth Saer]] roasts him for not worshiping Bahamut. The trio re-enter the tavern to talk further. After [[Callie O'Toole]] joins in the roast of [[Tharrick]], this time for being poor (before taking 130 gold for damages to the tavern), the three share drinks, and [[Tharrick]] rents out a room. [[Vaelithra]] fails the check against [[Callie O'Toole]]'s rizz and overpays for hers. Before departing, [[Vaelithra]] asks [[Tharrick]] why he's in [[Suzail]]; he's upfront about seeking The Hand. This nets confused looks. [[Vaelithra]] and [[Tanreth Saer]] then have a chat about her progress in the worship of Bahamut, which culminates into the advice of "do not ask, but seek". [[Tanreth Saer]] lets her know he's proud of her.

- The evening of Tarsakh 1 is upon us all, and the stooges ([[Ysgraviir]], [[Forge]], and [[Ven]]) are rotting in jail. [[Ven]] apologizes for his foolishness, and is fortunately forgiven, thought favors are most likely owed. Eventually, they hear footfalls and jangling keys in the distance. Purple Dragon Knights of the highest rank have arrived, with none other than [[Prince Erzoured Obarskyr]] in tow. [[Ysgraviir]] wastes no time in smarting off to the Prince, who responds in kind.

The Prince then reiterates the crimes the trio has committed, chiefly among them "trashing of a noble's name". As [[Prince Erzoured Obarskyr]] continues to verbally thrash the group, [[Ven]] pipes up and makes it clear that [[Ysgraviir]] and [[Forge]] had no ill intentions and did as they were instructed, taking full responsibility for the chaos at the cafe. After a tense discussion, it's established that the Prince actually agrees with [[Ven]]'s convictions about the lack of freedom that [[Lady Sarya Crownsilver]] suffers; he's merely pissed that [[Ven]] went about disparaging it so openly and stupidly. After assuring [[Ven]] that he now owes the Prince a favor, [[Prince Erzoured Obarskyr]] departs, to be quickly replaced with none other than [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]], who has posted the bail for the group. This comes at no small cost, however; after disparaging the crown a bit himself, the old elf provides a story about the importance of reputation, as it was relevant in his days among nobility, as he comes from high places himself; his noble line served as the Captains of the Elven Blades, the bladesinging King's Guard of Cormanthor. [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] beseeches [[Ysgraviir]], [[Forge]], and [[Ven]] to consider the good name of The Party, most of all his precious grandson [[Ascánthir]], before they get into trouble again. "At the very least, make sure you don't get caught!" After the trio is released and given their gear back, [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] takes [[Ven]] aside and essentially tells him to follow his heart, even if that means continuing to pursue [[Lady Sarya Crownsilver]] (but please don't be dumb about it). [[Ven]] assures him that he was briefly infatuated, but has no deep feelings for the woman; if anything, recent events have taught him to stay as far away from nobles of the fairer sex as humanly (tiefling-ly?) possible. They go their separate ways, but not before [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] relates to [[Ven]] a bit; he reveals that his daughter in law was not a sun elf, but a moon elf, which is considered quite taboo; paralleling [[Ven]]'s controversial courtship attempts.


- [[Ysgraviir]] returns to the Wide-Eyed Willow to mend fences. He is allowed to survey the damage to the cafe's namesake tree, which amounts to a few snapped off twigs. After enlisting help from [[Dahlia]], who was at the cafe, he offers 1 gold lion to the pixies on staff; they demand more, so he pays 10 and leaves for the [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]].

- [[Ember]] and [[Rook]] hit up the [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]], which is still cleaning up post-brawl. [[Callie O'Toole]] tries to upsell them on a fancier room, then explains the cause of the mess, pointing out [[Tharrick]], which is still sitting at a table. The couple employ some sneaking shenanigans, and scare the crap out of the Dragonborn. They quickly make nice and introduce each other. [[Tharrick]], after getting a cagey answer from [[Rook]] on the "what do you do for a living" icebreaker, takes him as a shady dude. [[Rook]] smooths this first impression over by offering some wine, which [[Tharrick]] accepts. [[Tharrick]] then throws all social graces out the window, asking directly about where he can find the Right Hand. After briefly considering skewering him on the spot, [[Rook]] says [[Tharrick]] may be interested in meeting the rest of The Party for more info; strength in numbers will be handy when dealing with the major problem that The Hand are turning out to be. Desiring rest, they depart to their rooms.

- [[Ascánthir]] heads to the [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]], [[Brannon]] in tow. He resists [[Callie O'Toole]]'s wiles, and pays for a room. [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] is already there, prompting [[Ascánthir]] to seek a refund for the room that clearly had been paid for already; he gets part of it back. Upon returning to the room, [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] accuses [[Ascánthir]] of being too cheap, eliciting an accusation from [[Ascánthir]] that he wouldn't have to be so stingy if he had access to the family coffers. Grandsire lets slip that he's been a bit spendy today, triggering further interrogation. [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] fesses up to buying a bunch of bits and bobs for the members of The Party, culminating in the presentation of a dingy necklace that [[Ascánthir]] nearly rejects, until he discovers it has valuable magical properties. [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] offers the reveal what he spent MOST of the money on today, but [[Ascánthir]] stops him, deciding he doesn't want to know. Interrogations aside, [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] begs [[Ascánthir]] to have a kid already, and is largely disregarded. [[Ascánthir]] then casts a Sending to his mother, indicating his arrival in [[Suzail]], Grandsire's wellbeing, and the desire to know how she's doing and where she is. The reply: "Hello dear, it is good to hear from you. I am in Shadowdale. Love you."

- [[Forge]] asks a Purple Dragon Knight where a good smithy is. The tired law enforcer asks if [[Forge]] is an honorable individual. Upon a positive answer, the Knight advises AGAINST the spa/smithy, advocating instead for a dwarf-staffed shop nearby. Staffed by a presumably married dwarven couple with a small girl helping out,the shop is approached by [[Forge]], requesting a shield upgrade. The male dwarf informs [[Forge]] it'll take 6 days and cost 1000 gold lions, but the cost can be brought down to 300 if he'll do some work. [[Forge]] asks if the price can be taken down to 200, prompting the dwarf to invite the giant into his office. The female dwarf whips up some food, then whisks the girl away to bed, leaving the male dwarf and [[Forge]] to talk business. The dwarf, introducing himself as Vance, informs [[Forge]] that a competing shop, A Softer Touch, is taking his business by selling mithril for absurdly cheap prices; as in, literally too cheap to be making money off of it. Vance makes it clear that he doesn't want the half-elf owner, Irelyn, to be harmed, but wants to know what the heck is up. Vance recommends [[Forge]] take his shield over there, find out what's up, then return for Vance to upgrade it at the low price of 200 gold lions. [[Forge]] departs for the competition.

- [[Ven]] visits a Temple to Oghma, wishing to return to his roots. Presenting his credentials to the Temple Overseer, he sits in on a literacy class being taught by an Orc named Tane. [[Ven]] helps teach for a bit, then seeks to unwind in the Millilian worship area of the temple. He elects to lounge in a chair and strum his lute for the first time in gods know how long. He closes his eyes, allowing the music to take him to peaceful places, when the temple suddenly becomes eerily silent, then explodes with screams. Startled, [[Ven]] opens his eyes to see a woman revealed to be [[Zabrina Carpenterbee]]. After a few minutes of sustained screamed adulation, the Temple Overseer kicks everyone out that isn't there to worship, which quiets things down. This prompts [[Ven]] to close his eyes and return to strumming. Eventually feeling a strange warmth on his eyelids, he opens and eye to see [[Zabrina Carpenterbee]] standing uncomfortably close, staring right at him. [[Ven]] asks what's up, prompting the woman to ask if he knows who she is. Upon an answer in the negative, [[Zabrina Carpenterbee]] is struck with excited relief, expressing how exhausting it is to be recognized and fawned over everywhere she goes. She asks [[Ven]] what sort of medium he practices the bardic arts in, and he responds that he prefers poetry. Being asked to read one, [[Ven]] recounts the story of the Death of the Green Death to her, prompting more delight. [[Zabrina Carpenterbee]] invites [[Ven]] to walk with her crew, as they're both going to The [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]], and [[Ven]] accepts, offering to share a drink. Upon leaving the Temple, they meet a tree that turns into a [[Dryad]], and a [[Satyr]]. [[Ven]] introduces himself, but is offered no names in return. They set off for the tavern.

- In the midst of getting some shuteye in their room, [[Ember]] and [[Rook]] are awakened by an attack: he is being stabbed, and she is being choked by a wire. The duo attempt to defend themselves, and chaos ensues. One of the assailants cast Darkness, which seeps into [[Tharrick]]'s adjacent room. Already having been woken up by the noise, he grabs a sword and shield and heads for the room. Finding the door locked, [[Tharrick]] bangs on it to no avail. Electing to make use of his Breath Weapon, [[Tharrick]] melts the lock and is greeted with the view of magical Darkness and the sound of struggle. At this point, [[Ascánthir]] is woken up by the ruckus, as his room is directly above. He can't make anything out through the floor, then heads for the room at [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]]'s insistence. Nightgowns a-flowing, they dart for the violence. [[Ascánthir]] throws what might as well be a magical flashbang into the fracas, blinding all but one assailant, and offering him the briefest glimpse of what the heck is going on in the room. This awakens [[Vaelithra]], who peers down the hallway to see the Darkness leaking out. [[Ember]] drops a magic sun nuke and deals some solid damage to the would-be assassins. In the confusion, [[Rook]] swings and misses a few times, but eventually finds purchase with his knife. [[Tharrick]] dishes out some much needed heals as [[Ascánthir]] does his best Leeroy Jenkins impression and comes in swangin'. He and [[Grandsire Na’thaniél]] swang and firebolt to similar levels of success. After taking some solid hits, the assailants grab onto some bracers on their right hands, and teleport out.

- [[Forge]] approaches A Softer Touch, which turns out to be open. After asking for shield improvements, Irelyn offers some suspiciously cheap pricing options, which [[Forge]] calls out as such. Irelyn misreads the accusation and offers to do it for even cheaper, furthering [[Forge]]'s confusion. [[Forge]] then accuses Irelyn of being too hot to be a blacksmith, which she accepts as a compliment, explaining that she does all her work with magic, down to using Mage Hand to handle equipment. When the subject of mithril comes up, Irelyn, again, offers it for way too cheap. [[Forge]] asks why she charges so little, and she explains that a lot of people will never have the money necessary to afford what other shops charge; she is able to do things for cheap because of her magical shortcuts, so she passes those saving on. The catch? Her enchantments are not cross-compatible: if you use her services once, you can't get upgrades done anywhere else. After chatting a bit more, Irelyn basically asks [[Forge]] out, and he doesn't decline.

- [[Forge]] departs for the [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]], and meets The Party sans [[Ven]] and plus [[Tharrick]] looking quite disheveled (that includes [[Callie O'Toole]]). Everyone is informed their rooms are free following the attempted assassination.

- [[Ven]] arrives at The [[Red Herring Inn & Tavern]] with his . . . crew. Introductions are made yet again. After some short conversation, [[Zabrina Carpenterbee]] and her band depart for the penthouse, but not before [[Ven]] offers her a poem as thanks for an interesting evening. She informs him she'll be performing later in the festival, and The Party should make their way to the front row at the show for a shout out. Right before leaving, [[Zabrina Carpenterbee]] makes mention of the "Hilp Six" name from the poem [[Ven]] read to her at the Temple. This prompts [[Tharrick]] to get very interested in the name of the group as it stands now. [[Forge]] notes [[Ven]]'s way with words, and the burden is laid upon him to come up with a name for The Party.
